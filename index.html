Frequently asked questions

What is JustOne?

JustOne is a campus-based introduction experience. Instead of endless swiping, we focus on one carefully considered match, based on compatibility, intent, and mutual interest.

How is this different from dating apps?

We don’t optimize for volume or engagement. There’s no feed, no swiping, and no pressure to keep browsing. The goal is a single, meaningful introduction — or none at all.

Who is this for?

JustOne is for students who are open to meeting someone intentionally, without the noise or performative nature of typical dating platforms.

How does matching work?

Participants answer a short questionnaire about values, intent, and preferences. Our system looks for the strongest overall compatibility within your campus. Matches are only revealed if interest is mutual.

Is this anonymous?

Yes, until a mutual match is confirmed. Your identity and responses are not shown to anyone unless both people opt in to the reveal.

What happens if I’m not matched?

Nothing is revealed. You won’t be notified of near-matches or partial results. We prioritize discretion over engagement.

When are matches revealed?

For this cycle, matches are revealed on February 14.

Is my data safe?

We collect only what’s necessary and do not sell or share personal data. This is a private pilot, and additional safeguards are being put in place before full rollout.

Is this a public launch?

No. This is a limited, invite-only pilot for select campuses.

Can I opt out later?

Yes. Participation is voluntary, and you can choose not to proceed at any stage.
